Workout & history folders
VeloDrive folder
All workouts, history, and trash live under this folder.
VeloDrive will store all of your data on your local filesystem: You only need to the choose the top-level folder, such as ~/Documents/velodrive. Inside the folder you choose, VeloDrive automatically creates: 
  ~/Documents/velodrive/
  ├── workouts/   ← your .zwo workout files
  ├── history/    ← completed workout .fit files
  └── trash/      ← deleted workouts
Workout behaviour
Functional Threshold Power (FTP)
W
FTP is your sustainable power for roughly a 1-hour effort. VeloDrive uses it to:
  • Convert relative workout segments (%FTP) into watts.
  • Calculate energy (kJ) consumed per workout.
If you’re unsure, start with a rough guess (e.g. last known FTP or outdoor 20–30 minute best power × 0.95) and adjust over time.
Interval sounds
When sounds are enabled:
  • Short beep pattern a few seconds before a big interval change.
  • More dramatic sound before very hard efforts.
  • Countdown & overlay when starting a workout.
This helps you prepare for intensity changes without staring at the screen.
Environment checks
Web Bluetooth
Web Bluetooth lets VeloDrive connect directly to your trainer and heart-rate monitor from the browser. It is supported only in Google Chrome on Linux, Windows, macOS, and Android. iOS browsers, Firefox, and some Chromium-based browsers (for example Vivaldi) do not support Web Bluetooth.

If Chrome doesn't detect your devices, you may need to enable its Web Bluetooth flags:

In Chrome on desktop, open a new tab and go to:
  • chrome://flags/#enable-web-bluetooth
  • chrome://flags/#enable-web-bluetooth-new-permissions-backend

Set both to Enabled, restart Chrome, then reload VeloDrive.
